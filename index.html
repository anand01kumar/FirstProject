Intro of Siddhartha Gautama Buddha

The Buddha was Siddhartha Gautama, the founder of Buddhism, who lived in northern India between the 6th and 4th centuries BCE:

Life

Born into a wealthy family, Gautama led an extravagant life until he became bored with the luxuries of royalty. He then left his palace to search for understanding, and came to believe that suffering was a part of all existence. He renounced his title and became a monk, practicing extreme self-discipline. After meditating under a tree for 49 days, he became the Buddha, or "Enlightened One".

Teachings

The Buddha taught the Four Noble Truths, which are a way to achieve peace of mind and happiness. He also described the Noble Eightfold Path, which includes right speech, right action, right livelihood, right effort, right mindfulness, and right concentration.
